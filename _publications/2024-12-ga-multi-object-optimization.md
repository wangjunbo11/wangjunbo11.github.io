## ⚙️ Background

In modern production systems, decision-making often involves **multiple conflicting objectives**, such as:

- improving inspection efficiency  
- reducing total cost  
- minimizing loss probability  

Traditional optimization methods struggle to handle such trade-offs simultaneously.

---

## 🧠 Methodology

This study proposes a **Genetic Algorithm (GA)-based multi-objective optimization framework**.

Key steps include:

- Bernoulli assumption for defective rate modeling  
- Binomial distribution + hypothesis testing for sampling design  
- Central Limit Theorem for minimum sample size determination  
- Construction of a multi-objective programming model  

The model introduces **dynamic inspection probability** as a decision variable to replace traditional binary (0–1) variables.

---

## 📊 Optimization Objectives

The model jointly optimizes:

- Maximization of inspection efficiency  
- Minimization of total inspection cost  
- Minimization of system loss probability  

---

## 🧪 Solution Strategy

Using GA’s global search capability, the model is solved under multiple scenarios.

Results include:

- optimal inspection schemes  
- cost comparisons across scenarios  
- sensitivity analysis of decision variables  

A further extension integrates **semi-finished product process optimization** using an ideal-point method combined with GA.

---

## 💰 Key Result

The optimal inspection scheme yields a minimum cost of:

> **3619.17**
