## 🌍 Background

With the rapid development of the digital economy and increasing concern over agricultural carbon emissions, understanding the environmental consequences of digital transformation has become critical for sustainable agricultural development.

---

## 📊 Data & Approach

This study uses **Chinese provincial panel data (2015–2022)** to construct a comprehensive digital economy index system.

A **fixed effects econometric model** is employed to identify the causal impact of digital economy development on agricultural carbon emissions.

---

## 📉 Main Findings

We find that:

> The development of the digital economy significantly reduces agricultural carbon emissions.

---

## 🔬 Mechanisms

The reduction effect operates through multiple channels:

- Optimization of industrial structure  
- Promotion of technological innovation  
- Improvement in service efficiency  

These mechanisms jointly contribute to the green transformation of agriculture.

---

## 🌱 Contribution

This paper contributes to the literature on:

- Digital economy and environmental sustainability  
- Agricultural carbon emission mitigation  
- Green transformation of rural economies  
- Empirical climate–economy interaction analysis  

---

## 🧠 Policy Implications

The results suggest that:

- Digital infrastructure investment can support carbon reduction goals  
- Rural digital transformation should be integrated into climate policy  
- Technology-driven agricultural modernization is key to sustainability transition
