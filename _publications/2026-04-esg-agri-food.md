## 🌍 Background

With intensifying climate change and increasing food safety concerns, global agri-food markets are facing stricter sustainability and quality requirements.

In this context, Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) performance has become a key driver of sustainable transformation in agri-food export systems.

---

## 📊 Main Findings

Using matched data from Chinese listed agri-food firms and China Customs, we find that:

> **ESG performance significantly promotes export product quality upgrading.**

This effect is economically and statistically significant, indicating that ESG acts as a structural driver of export upgrading rather than a peripheral factor.

---

## 🔬 Mechanisms

We identify two main channels through which ESG improves export quality:

- 🌱 **Green technological innovation**
- 🏷️ **Brand reputation enhancement**

These mechanisms jointly improve firms’ ability to move toward higher-quality export baskets.

---

## 📈 Heterogeneity Analysis

The ESG effect is not uniform. It is stronger for firms that:

- adopt **quality competition strategies**
- undergo **digital transformation**
- engage in **general trade (vs processing trade)**
- export to markets with **strict non-tariff barriers**

---

## 🌐 ESG Standard Coordination

We further examine international ESG disclosure frameworks and find that:

> Firms adopting the **Global Reporting Initiative (GRI)** standard are more likely to achieve export quality upgrading.

This highlights the importance of **standardization and comparability in ESG reporting systems**.

---

## 🌍 Contributions

This paper contributes to three strands of literature:

- ESG and corporate sustainability transformation  
- Trade upgrading and export quality literature  
- Climate-related institutional and governance frameworks  

---

## 🧠 Policy Implications

The findings provide insights for policymakers aiming to build **sustainable agri-food systems**:

- Strengthen ESG disclosure standards (GRI alignment)  
- Promote green innovation in agri-food firms  
- Encourage digital transformation in export sectors  
- Support quality-based trade upgrading strategies
